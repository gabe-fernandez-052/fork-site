How Fork works

1

Sign in, no password

Open Fork and you're greeted with one line: You nominate. They pick. Let's eat. Tap Apple or Google and you're in. Nothing to remember, nothing to type.

Sign in with Apple & Google
2

Nominate three spots

Search restaurants near you, dial in the distance and price, and line up your three picks — like Goin Coastal Seafood, Taco Mac, and Local on North. Then hit Send Fork.

Search · distance · price filters
3

Know before you nominate

Tap any spot for the full picture — a live Google map, rating, price, distance, "Open now," hours, and one-tap directions. Nominate with confidence.

Maps · ratings · hours
4

Send your Fork

Hit Share Link and your three nominations fly off to whoever you're eating with. The screen flips to Waiting… while they decide — no group-chat debate required.

One link. One tap.
5

They get the ping

If your friend has Fork, a push notification lands and their home screen lights up: A friend needs you to pick — tap to choose where you'll eat. No hunting through a chat thread.

Push notification → home card
6

They pick the winner

Your friend opens the link, swipes through your three, checks the map and hours, and taps Pick this restaurant. One decision, made in seconds.

Pick this restaurant
7

We're eating at…

Confetti, then the verdict — Taco Mac Canton. From the result screen, get directions, order delivery, or reserve a table. You both got the answer in seconds, not an hour of "I dunno, you pick."

Directions · delivery · reservations

Stop asking "where do you wanna eat?"
